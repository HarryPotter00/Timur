Nearly a decade after its 2012 launch, the Tesla Model S is getting a much-needed update for a 2021.5 or 2022 model year (Tesla doesn't follow model years as closely as other automakers). Two new three-engine variants are joining the lineup, while the exterior and interior design have been significantly redesigned for the better. This could be exactly what Tesla needs to stay relevant alongside popular newcomers, including the 1080hp Lucid Air Dream Edition. and Porsche Taycan Turbo S.
